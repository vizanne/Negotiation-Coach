✅ Before the Conversation
✅
I know my anchor rate and I'm ready to say it out loud
✅
I have 1–2 specific results ready to back up my rate
✅
I know my walk-away number — and I'm willing to use it
✅
I've practiced saying my rate without apologising or trailing off
✅
I'm prepared to be silent after I state my rate
🎯 During the Conversation
✅
Lead with value, not time — talk outcomes, not hours
✅
State your rate clearly — no "around" or "roughly"
✅
After saying your rate: stop talking. Let them respond first.
✅
If they push back: ask questions before conceding anything
✅
If you must move: reduce scope, don't slash your rate
✅
Get the agreed rate in writing before starting any work
✅ Things That Work
▼
✓
Name a specific number — "$1,200" lands better than "around $1,000–1,500"
✓
Anchor above your goal — you can always come down, never go up
✓
Ask for time to review — "Let me send you a formal quote by tomorrow"
✓
Reduce scope instead of rate — protect your hourly value
✓
Mention you have limited availability — scarcity is real leverage
✓
Always send a contract before starting — no exceptions
🚫 Things That Hurt You
▼
✗
Apologising for your rate — "Sorry, I know it's a lot, but..."
✗
Giving a range — they will always pick the bottom number
✗
Dropping your rate the moment they hesitate — wait for an actual counter
✗
Justifying your rate with your expenses or personal costs
✗
Working without a deposit — even for "quick" jobs
✗
Agreeing to extra work without agreeing to extra pay first
🧠 The Mindset Shift
