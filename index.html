✅ Before You Walk In
✓
I know my target range and opening number
✓
I have 2–3 specific wins / results ready to mention
✓
I've researched market rates on Glassdoor or LinkedIn
✓
I know my walk-away number and I'm okay with it
✓
I've practiced saying my number out loud (seriously, do this)
✓
I'm ready to be silent after I state my ask
✓
I have the objection responses ready
🎯 During the Negotiation
✓
Let them speak first if possible
✓
Never give a range first (they'll pick the bottom)
✓
Anchor HIGH — you can always come down, never go up
✓
After naming your number: STOP TALKING
✓
If they push back: "I understand — can you help me understand the constraints?"
✓
Negotiate benefits if salary is truly stuck (PTO, remote, title)
✓
Get any final offer in writing before you celebrate
✅ DO These Things
▼
✓
Express excitement about the role first, then negotiate
✓
Use specific numbers — "87,500" sounds more researched than "around 90k"
✓
Ask for 24–48 hours to consider a verbal offer
✓
Mention competing offers if you have them (early and confidently)
✓
Negotiate benefits — vacation, remote days, start date, signing bonus
✓
Send a follow-up email summarizing what was agreed
🚫 DON'T Do These
▼
✗
Say "I need this because of my bills / personal situation"
✗
Apologize for asking — "Sorry, but could I ask for..."
✗
Give a range when asked for a number (they pick the bottom)
✗
Accept on the spot — always ask for time to review
✗
Reveal your current salary if you're in a state where they can't ask
✗
Threaten to leave unless you are truly ready to walk
✗
Fill silence — say your number, then wait, even if it feels awkward
